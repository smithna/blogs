David Ferrer, the world’s fourth-ranked player, became the first high seed to tumble out of the men’s event at the BNP Paribas Open in Indian Wells, Calif., losing, 3-6, 6-4, 6-3, to the big-serving Kevin Anderson in the second round. Roger Federer advanced with a 6-2, 6-3 win over Denis Istomin, and No. 15 John Isner was defeated, 6-7 (6), 6-3, 6-4, by Lleyton Hewitt. Rafael Nadal, who is seeded fifth, moved into the third round with a 7-6 (3), 6-2 victory over Ryan Harrison.

In the women’s draw, the defending champion, Victoria Azarenka, overcame a 4-1 deficit in the first set to beat the two-time champion Daniela Hantuchova, 6-4, 6-1.
